Rome

The Eternal City

Rome, Italy's capital, is a sprawling, cosmopolitan city with nearly 3,000 years of globally influential art, architecture, and culture. Ancient ruins like the Colosseum and Roman Forum evoke the power of the former Roman Empire. Vatican City, headquarters of the Roman Catholic Church, has St. Peter's Basilica and the Vatican Museums.

Culture & Traditions

Roman culture is steeped in history, with influences from the ancient Roman Empire still visible today. The city is a living museum where ancient ruins stand alongside Renaissance palaces and Baroque fountains. Romans take pride in their heritage and maintain traditions like the passeggiata (evening stroll) and long, leisurely meals with family. The Catholic Church has profoundly influenced Roman culture, evident in the numerous churches and religious festivals. Art and architecture are everywhere - from Michelangelo's masterpieces to Bernini's sculptures.

Famous Places to Visit

Colosseum

An iconic symbol of Imperial Rome, this ancient amphitheater once held gladiatorial contests and could seat 50,000 spectators. It's one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world.

  • Piazza del Colosseo, 1, 00184 Roma
  • Daily 9:00 AM - 7:00 PM

Vatican Museums & Sistine Chapel

Home to one of the world's greatest art collections, including Michelangelo's breathtaking Sistine Chapel ceiling. The museums contain masterpieces spanning centuries of artistic achievement.

  • Viale Vaticano, 00165 Roma
  • Mon-Sat 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM

Trevi Fountain

The largest Baroque fountain in Rome, famous for the tradition of throwing coins to ensure a return to the city. The fountain is a masterpiece of sculpture and architecture.

  • Piazza di Trevi, 00187 Roma
  • 24/7

Pantheon

A former Roman temple, now a church, featuring the world's largest unreinforced concrete dome with an oculus opening to the sky. It's one of the best-preserved ancient Roman buildings.

  • Piazza della Rotonda, 00186 Roma
  • Mon-Sat 9:00 AM - 7:00 PM, Sun 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM
