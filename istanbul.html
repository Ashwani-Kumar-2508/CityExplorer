Istanbul

Where East Meets West

Istanbul is the only city in the world that straddles two continents - Europe and Asia. This ancient city, formerly known as Constantinople and Byzantium, has been the capital of three great empires. Today, it's a vibrant metropolis where minarets and church domes share the skyline, and ancient bazaars coexist with modern shopping districts.

Culture & Traditions

Istanbul's culture is a rich tapestry woven from Byzantine, Ottoman, and modern Turkish influences. The city's strategic location has made it a crossroads of civilizations for millennia. Turkish hospitality is legendary, with tea culture being central to social life. The call to prayer from hundreds of mosques creates a unique soundscape. Traditional arts like calligraphy, carpet weaving, and Turkish music thrive alongside contemporary arts. The hammam (Turkish bath) tradition continues. Despite modernization, Istanbul maintains strong connections to its layered past.

Cuisine & Food

Turkish cuisine is one of the world's great culinary traditions, blending Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, and Central Asian influences.

Kebabs

Various grilled meat dishes, from döner to shish kebab

Meze

Small appetizer dishes like hummus, dolma, and cacik

Baklava

Layered pastry with nuts and honey syrup

Simit

Sesame-crusted bread rings, a street food staple

Turkish Tea & Coffee

Strong black tea and thick, aromatic coffee

Famous Places to Visit

Hagia Sophia

A architectural marvel that has served as a church, mosque, museum, and now mosque again. Its massive dome and Byzantine mosaics are breathtaking.

  • Sultan Ahmet, Fatih, Istanbul
  • Open for prayer and visits (check times)

Blue Mosque (Sultan Ahmed Mosque)

Famous for its blue Iznik tiles and six minarets, this active mosque is one of Istanbul's most iconic landmarks.

  • At Meydanı No:7, Fatih, Istanbul
  • Open daily except during prayer times

Grand Bazaar

One of the world's oldest and largest covered markets, with over 4,000 shops selling carpets, jewelry, spices, ceramics, and more.

  • Beyazıt, Fatih, Istanbul
  • Mon-Sat 9:00 AM - 7:00 PM

Bosphorus Strait

The waterway separating Europe and Asia. Take a cruise to see palaces, fortresses, and mansions lining both shores.

  • Istanbul Strait
  • Cruises available throughout the day
