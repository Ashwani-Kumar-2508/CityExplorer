Cairo

Gateway to Ancient Egypt

Cairo, Egypt's sprawling capital, is set on the Nile River. At its heart is Tahrir Square and the vast Egyptian Museum. Nearby, Giza is the site of the iconic pyramids and Great Sphinx. Cairo is a city where ancient wonders meet modern urban life.

Culture & Traditions

Cairo's culture is deeply rooted in its ancient Egyptian heritage and Islamic traditions. The call to prayer echoes across the city five times daily. Egyptians are known for their warmth and hospitality. Family is central to life, and social gatherings often revolve around sharing meals. Cuisine & Food

Egyptian cuisine is flavorful and hearty, with influences from Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, and African cooking traditions.

Koshari

Egypt's national dish: rice, lentils, pasta, and tomato sauce

Ful Medames

Slow-cooked fava beans with olive oil and spices

Ta'ameya (Falafel)

Egyptian falafel made from fava beans

Mahshi

Vegetables stuffed with rice and herbs

Baklava

Sweet pastry with nuts and honey

Famous Places to Visit

Pyramids of Giza

The last remaining Wonder of the Ancient World, these 4,500-year-old pyramids are Egypt's most iconic monuments, including the Great Pyramid of Khufu.

  • Al Haram, Nazlet El-Semman, Giza
  • Daily 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM

Egyptian Museum

Home to the world's largest collection of ancient Egyptian artifacts, including the treasures of Tutankhamun's tomb.

  • Tahrir Square, Cairo
  • Daily 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Khan el-Khalili Bazaar

A historic marketplace dating back to the 14th century, offering spices, jewelry, textiles, and traditional crafts in a labyrinth of narrow alleys.

  • El-Gamaleya, Cairo
  • Daily 9:00 AM - 11:00 PM

Citadel of Saladin

A medieval Islamic fortification with stunning mosques, including the magnificent Muhammad Ali Mosque, offering panoramic views of Cairo.

  • Al Abageyah, Cairo
  • Daily 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM
