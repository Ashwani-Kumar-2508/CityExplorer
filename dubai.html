Dubai

City of Gold and Innovation

Dubai is a city of superlatives - home to the world's tallest building, largest shopping mall, and most luxurious hotels. This futuristic metropolis in the UAE has transformed from a desert trading port to a global business and tourism hub, blending ultramodern architecture with traditional Arabian culture.

Culture & Traditions

Dubai's culture is rooted in Islamic traditions and Bedouin heritage, while embracing modernity and multiculturalism. The city is home to over 200 nationalities. Traditional values of hospitality and respect are paramount. While cosmopolitan, Dubai maintains its cultural identity through souks, heritage sites, and cultural festivals. The city observes Islamic customs, with prayer times and Ramadan being significant. Despite rapid development, Emiratis preserve their traditions through falconry, camel racing, and traditional dress.

Famous Places to Visit

Burj Khalifa

The world's tallest building at 828 meters, offering breathtaking views from its observation decks on the 124th and 148th floors.

  • 1 Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Blvd, Dubai
  • Daily 8:30 AM - 11:00 PM

Dubai Mall

One of the world's largest shopping malls, featuring over 1,200 stores, an aquarium, ice rink, and the famous Dubai Fountain.

  • Financial Centre Road, Dubai
  • Daily 10:00 AM - 12:00 AM

Palm Jumeirah

An artificial archipelago in the shape of a palm tree, home to luxury hotels, residences, and the famous Atlantis resort.

  • Palm Jumeirah, Dubai
  • 24/7

Dubai Creek & Gold Souk

Historic waterway lined with traditional souks selling gold, spices, and textiles. Take an abra (water taxi) for an authentic experience.

  • Deira, Dubai
  • Souk: Sat-Thu 10:00 AM - 10:00 PM
