Tokyo

Where Tradition Meets Future

Tokyo, Japan's bustling capital, mixes the ultramodern and the traditional, from neon-lit skyscrapers to historic temples. The city is known for its vibrant pop culture, cutting-edge technology, world-class dining, and beautiful cherry blossoms.

Explore More Sites

Culture & Traditions

Tokyo's culture is a fascinating blend of ancient traditions and modern innovation. The city honors its samurai heritage and traditional arts like tea ceremonies, calligraphy, and kabuki theater, while simultaneously being at the forefront of technology, fashion, and pop culture. Respect, punctuality, and attention to detail are deeply ingrained values. The concept of 'omotenashi' (wholehearted hospitality) is evident everywhere.

Cuisine & Food

Tokyo is a food lover's paradise with more Michelin-starred restaurants than any other city. From street food to haute cuisine, the variety is astounding.

Famous Places to Visit

Senso-ji Temple

Tokyo's oldest and most significant temple, located in Asakusa. The approach to the temple is through the iconic Kaminarimon Gate and Nakamise shopping street.

  • 2 Chome-3-1 Asakusa, Taito City, Tokyo
  • Daily 6:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Tokyo Skytree

The tallest structure in Japan at 634 meters, offering spectacular 360-degree views of Tokyo from its observation decks.

  • 1 Chome-1-2 Oshiage, Sumida City, Tokyo
  • Daily 10:00 AM - 9:00 PM

Shibuya Crossing

The world's busiest pedestrian crossing, a symbol of Tokyo's energy and modernity. Surrounded by massive video screens and neon lights.

  • Shibuya City, Tokyo
  • 24/7

Meiji Shrine

A serene Shinto shrine dedicated to Emperor Meiji and Empress Shoken, surrounded by a beautiful forest in the heart of the city.

  • 1-1 Yoyogikamizonocho, Shibuya City, Tokyo
  • Daily sunrise to sunset
