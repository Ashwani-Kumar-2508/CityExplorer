London

A City of History and Innovation

London, the capital of England and the United Kingdom, is a 21st-century city with history stretching back to Roman times. At its center stand the imposing Houses of Parliament, the iconic 'Big Ben' clock tower, and Westminster Abbey. The city seamlessly blends historic landmarks with modern architecture.

Culture & Traditions

London's culture is a rich tapestry woven from centuries of history and waves of immigration. The city is a global center for arts, fashion, finance, and media. British traditions like afternoon tea, pub culture, and the Royal Family coexist with cutting-edge contemporary art and diverse music scenes. Londoners value politeness, queuing, and a good sense of humor. The city's world-class museums, theaters, and galleries make it a cultural powerhouse.

Famous Places to Visit

Big Ben & Houses of Parliament

The iconic clock tower and Gothic Revival palace housing the UK Parliament, symbols of British democracy and architectural grandeur.

  • Westminster, London SW1A 0AA
  • Tours available, check website for times

Tower of London

A historic castle founded in 1066, home to the Crown Jewels and centuries of fascinating, often dark, British history.

  • St Katharine's & Wapping, London EC3N 4AB
  • Tue-Sat 9:00 AM - 5:30 PM

British Museum

One of the world's greatest museums, housing artifacts from human history and culture, including the Rosetta Stone and Egyptian mummies.

  • Great Russell St, London WC1B 3DG
  • Daily 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Buckingham Palace

The official London residence of the British monarch, famous for the Changing of the Guard ceremony and State Rooms open to visitors in summer.

  • Westminster, London SW1A 1AA
  • State Rooms open July-September
